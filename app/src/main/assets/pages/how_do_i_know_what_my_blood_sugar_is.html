When to check blood sugar

Checking blood sugar is important because it gives us a “roadmarker” of what to do for our blood sugar next—do I need a correction? Do I need to treat a low blood sugar? Is it safe to exercise?

  • Before meals
  • Bedtime
  • 2 am (for a little while when first diagnosed—your diabetes doctor will tell you when to stop checking at 2am)
  • Before exercising
  • When experiencing symptoms of low or high blood sugar
  • When sick

Steps to check blood sugar

  • Gather supplies
    1)Meter 2) Lancets 3) Test strips
  • Wash hands with soap and water, then dry.
  • Place test strip in meter
  • Hold the lancing device to side of finger; press button to stick.
  • Apply blood to the strip according to meter directions.
  • Look at blood sugar result on meter and record result.
  • If hyperglycemia, see “giving insulin to bring down high blood sugars” in “how to calculate insulin dosing” section
